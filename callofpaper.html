Call For Papers

Thematic Areas

We invite contributions in the form of original research papers, case studies, and conceptual frameworks on the following indicative themes:

  • Medicine & Health
  • Education: Learning, Culture & Social Transformation
  • Applied Anthropology & Public Engagement
  • Development, SDGs, Policy & Practice
  • Digital Space & Virtual Ethnography
  • Urban Dynamics
  • Environment & Climate Change
  • Gender in Contemporary Practice
  • Museums, Heritage & Arts
  • Open Theme – Any other area aligned with the seminar

Participation Guidelines

For Academic Participants:

  • Research Papers: (3000 - 5000 words)
  • Poster Presentations
  • Thesis presentations

For Non-Governmental Organizations:

  • Case Study Presentations
  • Best Practice Sharing Sessions
  • Community Experience Narratives

For Policymakers:

  • Policy Brief Presentations
  • Policy-Practice Interface Sessions

Awards:

Best Paper

Under each technical session, two best presentation award certificates will be given, one for category-A (Students and Research Scholars) and the other for category-B (Academicians and Researchers)

Submission Guidelines

Abstract Submission Requirements:

  • Length: 250-300 words
  • Format: Single-spaced, 12-point font, 1-inch margins
  • Content: Research question, methodology, key findings, significance
  • Keywords: 4-6 relevant keywords
  • Author Information: Name, affiliation, contact details, brief bio.

Full Paper Requirements (if accepted):

  • Research Papers: 3000-5000 words excluding references
  • Format: APA style citations and references
