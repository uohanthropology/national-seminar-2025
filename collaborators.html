Indira Gandhi Rashtriya Manav Sangrahalaya (IGRMS), Bhopal, INDIA

The Indira Gandhi Rashtriya Manav Sangrahalaya (IGRMS), located in Bhopal, Madhya Pradesh, is a premier national museum of anthropology under the Ministry of Culture, Government of India. Established in 1977 and spread over 200 acres of the prehistoric Shamla Hills, it is one of the largest anthropological museums in India. IGRMS uniquely combines eight open-air exhibitions and twelve indoor galleries, curated to depict the story of humankind across time and space, with a special focus on India‚Äôs tribal and rural communities.

The museum also conducts workshops, seminars, cultural festivals, artist camps, and community engagement programs. Its reference library supports research in anthropology, archaeology, tribal studies, museology, art, and architecture.

Prof. Amitabh Pande, Director & CEO, IGRMS, Bhopal,
Ministry of Culture, Government of India.

Sthreesakthi Private Ltd., Hyderabad

Sthreesakthi Private Limited is a social enterprise committed to empowering farmers, particularly those from marginalised communities, through innovative and sustainable livelihood solutions. Established in 2015, it emerged as an offshoot of the Hyderabad-based Gamana Voluntary Organisation, founded by Anil Dayakar Cheruku.

Sthreesakthi acts as a Special Purpose Vehicle (SPV) to support Farmer Producer Companies (FPCs) across various constituencies. Its mission focuses on enabling farmers to adopt technologies in renewable energy (like solar power) and modern agriculture (such as hydroponics).

Mr. Anil Kumar Cheruku, Chairman & MD,
Sthreesakthi Private Ltd., Hyderabad.
