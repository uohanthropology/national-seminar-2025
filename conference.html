Background

Anthropology today engages directly with health inequities, educational transformation, environmental risk, digital life, urban change, and heritage futures. Bringing scholars, practitioners, policymakers, and communities into one forum enables problem-focused, context-sensitive knowledge that can inform policy and practice while strengthening university–society linkages. This concept note is derived from the final public note issued by the organising team.

Objectives

  • - Convene diverse stakeholders to examine how anthropological perspectives and methods address contemporary societal challenges across education, health, environment, technology, and urban development.
  • - Surface actionable insights, methods, and collaborations for field programmes, teaching, and policy engagement.
  • - Create avenues for student learning, practitioner exchange, and community voice.

Key Focus Areas

  • Medicine and Health
  • Education: Learning, Culture, and Social Transformation
  • Applied Anthropology and Public Engagement
  • Development, SDGs, Policy and Practice
  • Digital Space and Virtual Ethnography
  • Urban dynamics
  • Environment and Climate Change
  • Gender in contemporary practice
  • Museums, Heritage and Arts
  • Any other area related to the theme of the seminar is also welcome

Target Audience

  • - Researchers in anthropology and other social sciences
  • - Graduate and Undergraduate Students pursuing anthropological studies
  • - Practitioners in applied anthropology, development work, and social services
  • - Policymakers in education, health, urban planning, and environmental sectors
  • - Community Leaders, practitioners and Students.
  • - NGO Representatives and social activists
  • - Corporate Professionals in organisational development and market research
  • - Digital Technology Specialists interested in cultural aspects of technology

Expected Outcomes

Actionable Insights
 To generate practical insights, methods, and collaborations that can be applied to field programs, teaching, and policy engagement.

Avenues for Learning
 To create opportunities for student learning, practitioner exchange, and community members to voice their perspectives.

Published Papers
 Selected papers from the conference will be published in the conference proceedings, providing an official record of the research presented.
