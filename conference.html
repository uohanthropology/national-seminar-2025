About Conference

Background

In the 21st century, anthropology is increasingly positioned as a vital discipline for navigating the complex intersections of culture, technology, environment, health, and governance. Its commitment to holistic, context-sensitive inquiry offers unique tools for understanding societal challenges and fostering sustainable futures.

As global and local contexts face rapid transformations, from digital disruption and climate change to educational inequalities and public health crises, anthropologists are called to bridge theory and practice, academia and community, policy and lived realities. The contemporary practice of anthropology requires engaged scholarship, interdisciplinary collaboration, and innovative methodologies that respond to both urgent problems and long-term societal shifts.

This seminar aims to provide a platform for scholars, practitioners, and community stakeholders to engage in dialogue, share empirical insights, and co-develop pathways that align anthropological expertise with the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), public policy priorities, and grassroots needs.

Objectives

  • To critically examine the evolving role of anthropology in addressing contemporary global and local challenges.
  • To facilitate cross-sectoral engagement between academia, policymakers, practitioners, and community leaders to evolve sustainable pathways.
  • To generate policy-relevant and practice-oriented recommendations rooted in anthropological evidence and methods.
  • To showcase innovative research, applied interventions, and collaborative projects in anthropology and allied fields.

Thematic Areas

  • Medicine and Health
  • Education: Learning, Culture, and Social Transformation
  • Applied Anthropology and Public Engagement
  • Development, SDGs, Policy and Practice
  • Digital Space and Virtual Ethnography
  • Urban dynamics
  • Environment and Climate Change
  • Gender in contemporary practice
  • Museums, Heritage and Arts
  • Any other area related to the theme of the seminar is also welcome

Target Audience

  • Researchers in anthropology and other social sciences
  • Graduate and Undergraduate Students pursuing anthropological studies
  • Practitioners in applied anthropology, development work, and social services
  • Policymakers in education, health, urban planning, and environmental sectors
  • Community Leaders, practitioners and Students.
  • NGO Representatives and social activists
  • Corporate Professionals in organisational development and market research
  • Digital Technology Specialists interested in cultural aspects of technology

Expected Outcomes

Conference Proceedings Volume

A curated compilation of selected, peer-reviewed papers presented at the seminar, serving as a lasting scholarly resource.

Collaborative Networks

New avenues for research and practice collaborations across institutions, disciplines, and sectors, fostering interdisciplinary engagement.

Policy Recommendations

Development of concise policy briefs and actionable recommendations aimed at informing government bodies, non-governmental organizations, and community stakeholders.

Visibility for Applied Anthropology

Enhanced recognition of applied anthropology and community-engaged scholarship in India and internationally, positioning the discipline as a critical contributor to contemporary societal challenges.

Significance

This seminar positions anthropology not merely as an academic discipline but as a practice-oriented field capable of shaping inclusive policies, sustainable livelihoods, cultural resilience, and innovative responses to contemporary challenges. It will be a forum for critical reflection, knowledge co-production, and strengthening the anthropological community’s role in building a sustainable future.
