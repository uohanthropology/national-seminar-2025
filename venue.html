Hyderabad

Hyderabad, known as the “City of Pearls,” is a vibrant blend of heritage and modernity. Founded in 1591 by Muhammad Quli Qutb Shah, the city is famed for its iconic Charminar, Golconda Fort, and the magnificent Qutb Shahi Tombs. A historical hub of culture, art, and architecture, Hyderabad has long been a meeting point of North and South India.

Hyderabad is a global hub for information technology and pharmaceuticals, known as "Cyberabad". The city blends tradition, with its bustling bazaars, and innovation, with its cutting-edge tech parks. It is also a paradise for food lovers, famous for its Hyderabadi Biryani and Irani Chai.

As a venue for the National Seminar 2025, Hyderabad offers delegates not only an engaging academic atmosphere but also an immersive cultural experience, where centuries of history and contemporary dynamism converge.

University of Hyderabad

The University of Hyderabad(UoH) is a premier central university established in 1974. Recognized for its academic excellence and research, the university is located on a lush 2,300-acre campus that provides a serene environment for intellectual pursuits and is surrounded by rich biodiversity.

UoH is consistently ranked among India's top universities and has been designated an Institute of Eminence by the government. The university is known for its strong interdisciplinary programs, cutting-edge research across science, technology, social sciences, humanities, and arts, and its emphasis on critical thinking and innovation.

With its world-class faculty, advanced research facilities, and diverse student body, UoH remains a hub for creating and sharing knowledge. The university's decision to host the National Seminar 2025 demonstrates its commitment to promoting academic discussion and contributing to policy and societal change.

Department of Anthropology

Established in 1979, the Department of Anthropology at the University of Hyderabad is a leading centre for teaching and research in both Biological and Social-Cultural Anthropology.

The department is known for its work in tribal and rural studies, medical anthropology, gender, environment, urban dynamics, forensic science, and human genetics. Its programs — from M.A. and M.Sc. to Ph.D. — blend classroom learning with field-based research.

Alumni of the department contribute significantly in academia, policy, and applied research. Hosting the National Seminar 2025 reflects its commitment to engaging with contemporary challenges and fostering dialogue for a sustainable future.
